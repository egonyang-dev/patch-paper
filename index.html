01

What remains after the fire.

Not the object, but the outline it left behind. A patch is not a repair — it is a testimony. It marks where something was missing, and chooses to stay visible rather than hide.

Material study, 2024. Mixed media on Japanese tissue paper.

02

The archive is always burning.

Every document is already decaying at the moment of its creation. To preserve is to choose what is allowed to be forgotten and what must be held, however imperfectly.

03

Non-linear reading.

These pages were not written in order. They were found, gathered, placed beside each other. Meaning emerges from proximity, not sequence. You are invited to begin anywhere.

Process

How a patch is made.

I

Collect

Fragments of text, material, gesture. Nothing is too small.

II

Damage

Introduce absence. Tear, burn, dissolve. The gap is part of the work.

III

Patch

Repair with a different material. Do not match. Let the seam show.

IV

Place

Position within an archive. Assign a number. Allow it to be incomplete.

04

The paper remembers the pressure.

Even after the ink is gone, the surface holds the ghost of what was written. This is not metaphor. This is paper.
